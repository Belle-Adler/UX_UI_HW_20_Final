Planly App Design

Belinda Adler, Jeffrey Boehme and Olga Peshkova

Objective

Our objective was to build an app that would help users overcome one of their biggest painpoints when planning a trip - staying organised and ensuring they have all tehir required needs.

Role

User Research, UX/UI Designer

Tools Used

Miro, Figma

Time Frame

3 weeks

User Research

Our research methodology utilised one on one Qualitative Interviews, these were roughly 10-20 minutes in length with 6 interviews conducted over one day by 3 separate interviewers. The interviews were a mix of in person and online interviews (using Zoom).

After conducting the interviews, we grouped similar issues participant experienced, identifying 4 key problem areas users had when travelling.

  • Budget
  • Tools
  • Planning
  • Research

We decided to focus mainly around the planning stage of users journey as this was an area that seemed to affect the majority of users which meant we felt this app could be beneficial to a large number of users.

Problem Statement

Planly was designed to help users plan and organise their trips. We have observed that users find planning overwhelming and time consuming, which is causing potential travellers to have stressful and frustrating experiences.

Next, we moved onto the ideation phase, utilising the I like, I wish, what if Method.


I like
  • Keeping everything centralised
  • To be able to easily keep track of my information
  • Plan my trips early
I wish
  • I didn't have to think about as many different parts of my planning
  • It was less time consuming to plan my travel
  • My friends could collaborate easily when travelling together
What If
  • A.I. planned my trip, based on my interests and budget
  • I could test the resort before staying there
  • I could plan my whole trip from one place

From here, we moved onto identifyfing the core issue or users faced and how our app could overcome this. Once we had an idea of the features the app would have, we storyboarded a customer journey with Emily a 25 year old travelleing abroad with friends.

Competitor Analysis

Purpose

A free app that provides users with a checklist when packing their belongings with 418 ratings for an average score of 4.16.

Advantages

This app was easy to use with simple graphics to convey information. The free version also required no onboarding, limiting users barrier to entry. The key advantage was around users ability to personalise their experiences.

Disadvantages

One of the most frequent criticisms was around the fact that deleted items don't syat deleted and the update quantity function was qute clunkly. Users also reported issues around device synchronisation.

Purpose

Check my trip is a free app that helps users book trips, parking, rent cars and track the status of their flight. With 839 ratings who scored the app a 4.5 on average.

Advantages

Its key features is that it emails users their booking confirmations as well as its ability to keep of track of many parts of journey together in one spot (flights, hotels, activities, parking & transfers.

Disadvantages

There were a few significant disadvantages we uncovered including; in-depth terms of service that was confusing, invasive requests of data. The scanning feature was unreliable with a poor interface for tracking trips.

Purpose

TripIt was all your trips kept in one app, instantly creating iteneries and real time flight alerts. This app had the most users reviews with over 30k with the highest score of 4.7

Advantages

Its ability to invite other users to view/share the trip bookings/reservations was its biggest advantage, in addition to the ability to keep all documemts in one centralised place. Trip It has huge following with already 5M downloads.

Disadvantages

However, there were a few clear disadvantages - the app required users to provide permission to scan all your emails for relevent info which alot of users could find invasive. There is also a lot of forms to fill in and can be difficult to make changes.

As a result of these usability tests, we identified 3 key areas that required Iteration: the onboarding process, readibility and functionality:
Onboarding
  • Fixed tutorials - added context for clarity
  • Fixed login screen spacing
  • Clarified login screen
  • Fixed Register Button
Readibility
  • Updated font contrasts
  • Changed flow from list to packing list
  • Distinguished edit buttons
Functionality
  • Updated location permissions
  • Updated permission prompts and removed ? symbol
  • Added date range instead of leaving date
  • Added reorder functionality to list
  • Added share buttons
